Robots in Warehousing:

Revolutionising Efficiency and Productivity

Choices

Ethical

  • Job Retraining: Companies can invest in job retraining programs to enable a smoother transition, ensuring employees are equipped with the necessary skills to continue their careers. (De Koster, 2018)
  • Collaboration: Companies can collaborate with unions and worker representivies to address the ethical implications of job displacement caused by the automation of warehouse activities.(De Koster, 2018)

Economical

  • Cost-Benefit Analysis: Companies can conduct a cost-benefit analysis to assess the economic impact of robot integration in warehousing, guiding decision-making for sustainable growth and profitability.(Mirzaei et al., 2022)
  • Partnerships: Companies can partner with government agencies and non-profit organisations to access funding and resources for implementing more sustainable practices, transitioning towards socially responsible operations. (Mirzaei et al., 2022)

Environmental

  • Sustainable Design: Companies can prioritise the design and implementation of energy-efficient robots made from sustainable materials. (Peterson et al., n.d.)
  • Recycling: Companies can implement sustainable disposal and recycling methods for electronic waste generated by robots. (Peterson et al., n.d.)
