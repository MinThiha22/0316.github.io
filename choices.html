CTiS: Robots in Warehousing

Choices

Cultural

  • Job Retraining: Companies can invest in job retraining programs to help replaced workers transition into new roles.
  • Collaboration: Companies can work with unions and worker representatives to address the ethical implications of job displacement.

Economical

  • Cost-Benefit Analysis: Companies can conduct a cost-benefit analysis to determine the long-term economic impact of implementing robots in warehousing.
  • Partnerships: Companies can partner with government agencies and non-profit organizations to access funding and resources for implementing sustainable practices.

Environmental

  • Sustainable Design: Companies can design and implement robots that are energy-efficient and made from sustainable materials.
  • Recycling: Companies can implement sustainable disposal and recycling methods for electronic waste generated by robots.
