Robots in Warehousing:

Revolutionising Efficiency and Productivity

Opportunities

Increased Efficiency

Robots have an ability to operate continuously without rest or breaks, and can manage different tasks faster and more precise compared to humans, resulting in higher productivity and efficiency. This can lead to reduced labour costs.
(Mirzaei et al., 2022)

Scalability

Robots can easily be added or removed from the warehouse in response to changing demand, allowing for flexible and scalable operations.
(De Koster, 2018)

Cost Savings

Robots can reduce labour costs by replacing human workers for repetitive and physically demanding tasks freeing up employees for higher-value work.
(De Koster, 2018)

Improved Safety

Robots can perform dangerous tasks and work in hazardous environments, reducing the risk of workplace accidents and injuries for human workers. (De Koster, 2018)

Enhanced Accuracy

Robots can operate with a high degree of accuracy and consistency, reducing errors and improving inventory management, production and order fulfilment, and resulting in improved overall performance. (Mirzaei et al., 2022)
